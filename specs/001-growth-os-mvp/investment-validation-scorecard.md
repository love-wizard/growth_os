# Investment Validation Scorecard: Growth OS v0.1 MVP

## Purpose

This scorecard turns Growth OS v0.1 into a validation MVP. It defines the minimum evidence needed before expanding features, widening the target audience, or presenting Growth OS as a broader family growth system.

The main hypothesis:

> High-pain parents will use child-specific AI companionship guidance during a real family friction moment and convert that guidance into an actual parent-child action.

Everything in v0.1 must support this hypothesis. Features that do not improve the scorecard should wait.

## Private Beta Design

Pilot size:

- 30 target families

Pilot duration:

- 14 days

Required family profile:

- Child age 4-7
- At least one ongoing interest or habit direction
- At least one parent reports a real companionship friction from the last 30 days
- Parent agrees to try at least one AI suggestion during the pilot

Priority friction segments:

1. Piano or interest-practice conflict
2. Reading habit interruption
3. Unclear English exposure
4. Limited evening time for companionship

Avoid recruiting broad "education-interested" families without a recent friction. They may give positive concept feedback without proving urgency or willingness to pay.

## Primary Loop

The scorecard tracks one loop:

1. Parent has a real friction
2. Parent generates a child-specific suggestion
3. Parent accepts or saves the suggestion
4. Parent tries the action with the child
5. Product creates or offers a lightweight growth record draft
6. Parent returns naturally or through a warm reminder
7. Parent states whether they would pay for a specific package

Warm reminders, expert review, weekly plans, and growth records are supporting mechanisms. They are not independent success stories unless they strengthen this loop.

## Scorecard Metrics

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Qualified high-pain family share | 80% of pilot families | Ensures feedback comes from urgent users, not casual concept testers. |
| First guidance completion rate | 60% | Proves onboarding is light enough to reach first value. |
| First suggestion acceptance or save rate | 40% | Proves the first answer is credible enough to preserve or try. |
| Seven-day companionship action rate | 25% | Proves advice becomes real parent-child behavior. |
| Growth record draft creation rate | 20% | Proves action can become memory without heavy manual recording. |
| Organic second-week return rate | 20% | Separates intrinsic demand from notification-driven traffic. |
| Total second-week return rate | 30% | Measures overall return including warm reminders. |
| Warm-reminder downstream action rate | 20% of opened reminders | Proves reminders create useful action, not just opens. |
| Generic AI comparison win rate | 60% | Proves Growth OS beats a generic chatbot for the same challenge. |
| Expert-reviewed trust lift | Positive lift in trust feedback | Tests whether expert calibration increases confidence without becoming the product. |
| Payment intent at RMB 19/29/49 per month | 15% strong intent | Tests whether usage value can become commercial value. |
| Parent pressure sentiment | 80% supportive or acceptable | Protects the anti-anxiety product promise. |

## Payment Test

The pilot should test willingness to pay before building commercial workflows.

Package concepts:

1. Basic: AI companionship suggestions + growth archive
2. Plus: AI companionship suggestions + growth archive + monthly growth analysis
3. High-trust: AI companionship suggestions + expert-reviewed guidance samples

Price points to test:

- RMB 19 per month
- RMB 29 per month
- RMB 49 per month

Required questions:

- Which package would you consider paying for?
- At which price would you hesitate?
- Would expert-reviewed guidance make you more willing to pay?
- Would you recommend this to parents with a child in the same age range?

Do not treat "I like it" as payment validation. Strong payment intent means the parent selects a package and a price they would realistically pay.

## Organic vs Reminder-Driven Return

Investor concern:

> Notifications may hide weak intrinsic demand.

Therefore the pilot must split:

- Organic second-week return: parent returns without a reminder click
- Reminder-driven return: parent returns after opening a warm reminder
- Reminder downstream action: parent completes a meaningful action after reminder entry
- Reminder opt-out or negative feedback: parent disables reminders or reports pressure

If organic return is weak but reminder-driven return is strong, Growth OS should keep testing the reminder mechanism but should not claim strong natural habit formation.

## Expert Trust Test

Expert involvement must be tested without turning Growth OS into a consulting marketplace.

Allowed pilot treatments:

- AI-only answer
- Expert-reviewed label on a sampled AI answer
- Asynchronous expert answer for a small number of selected questions

Measure:

- Parent trust rating
- Preference vs AI-only answer
- Willingness to pay for expert-reviewed guidance
- Whether expert involvement creates expectations of real-time support

Kill signal:

- Parents only trust the human expert and do not trust the AI-memory loop
- Expert demand requires real-time operations
- Safety-sensitive questions dominate expert requests

## Go / No-Go Rules

Proceed to broader MVP implementation if:

- First guidance completion, suggestion acceptance, action completion, and second-week return reach target or near-target levels
- Growth OS beats generic AI in side-by-side comparison
- Payment intent is visible in at least one package
- Parent pressure sentiment remains acceptable

Iterate the wedge if:

- Parents like the concept but do not act
- Suggestions are accepted but not tried
- Reminder-driven return works but organic return is weak
- Payment intent is unclear

Pause expansion if:

- Advice is repeatedly described as generic
- Fewer than 15% complete a companionship action within 7 days
- Organic second-week return is near zero
- Parents say the product feels like another task
- Expert layer becomes necessary for basic trust

## Scope Control Rule

Any proposed v0.1 feature must answer one of these:

- Does it increase first guidance completion?
- Does it increase suggestion acceptance?
- Does it increase actual companionship action?
- Does it increase organic second-week return?
- Does it prove Growth OS is better than generic AI?
- Does it clarify willingness to pay?
- Does it reduce parent pressure or trust risk?

If not, defer it.
